## Chapter 4 Introduction to spectral theory (eigenvalues and eigenvectors)

### 1. Main definitions

> 1.7. Show that characteristic polynomial of a block triangular matrix $$ \begin{pmatrix} 
    A & * \\ \mathbf{0} & B
\end{pmatrix}, $$ where $A$ and $B$ are square matrices, coincides with $\det(A − \lambda I)\det(B − \lambda I)$.

$$ \begin{aligned}
 \det\begin{pmatrix} 
    A-\lambda I & * \\ \mathbf{0} & B-\lambda I
\end{pmatrix} &= \det\begin{pmatrix} 
    I & * \\ \mathbf{0} & B-\lambda I
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 
    A-\lambda I & \mathbf{0} \\ \mathbf{0} & I
\end{pmatrix}\\
    & = \det(A-\lambda I)\det(B-\lambda I)
\end{aligned} $$ 

> 1.8. Let $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \ldots , \mathbf{v}_n$ be a basis in a vector space $V$. Assume also that the first $k$ vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \mathbf{v}_2, \ldots , \mathbf{v}_k$ of the basis are eigenvectors of an operator $A$, corresponding to an eigenvalue $\lambda$ (i.e. that $A\mathbf{v}_j = \lambda \mathbf{v}_j , j = 1, 2, \ldots , k$). Show that in this basis the matrix of the operator $A$ has block triangular form $$ \begin{pmatrix} 
    \lambda I_k & * \\ \mathbf{0} & B 
\end{pmatrix},  $$ where $I_k$ is $k \times k$ identity matrix and $B$ is some $(n − k) \times (n − k)$ matrix.

设这 $k$ 个向量张成子空间 $W$，由 $A\mathbf{v}_j = \lambda \mathbf{v}_j$，$W$ 为 $A$ 的不变子空间，分块矩阵左下角为 $\mathbf{0}$，显然左上角为 $\lambda I_k$，由维数知 $B$ 为 $(n − k) \times (n − k)$.

> 1.9. Use the two previous exercises to prove that geometric multiplicity of an eigenvalue cannot exceed its algebraic multiplicity.

设 $A \in \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{F}^n)$，$\lambda_k$ 为 $A$ 的一个特征值，几何重数为 $k$，即 $\dim \operatorname{Ker} (A-\lambda_k I) = k$，由 (1.8.)，$A$ 可表示为分块三角矩阵 $\mathcal{M}(A)$，由 (1.7.)，$\mathcal{M}(A)$ 特征多项式为 $(\lambda_k-\lambda)^k\det(B-\lambda I)$，右侧的 $\det(B-\lambda I)$ 仍可能含有 $\lambda_k$ 项，故算术重数大于等于 $k$.
（参照 LADR 定理 8.31, 9.62，代数重数另可定义为 $\dim\operatorname{Ker}(A-\lambda I)^{\dim V}$）

### 2. Diagonaliztion.

> 2.9 Let us recall that the famous Fibonacci sequence: $0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, \ldots $ is defined as follows: we put $\phi_0 = 0, \phi_1 = 1$ and define $\phi_{n+2} = \phi_{n+1} + \phi_n$. We want to find a formula for $\phi_n$. To do this
>> a) Find a $2 \times 2$ matrix $A$ such that \[ \begin{pmatrix} 
    \phi_{n+2} \\ \phi_{n+1} 
\end{pmatrix} = A \begin{pmatrix} 
    \phi_{n+1} \\ \phi_n 
\end{pmatrix}.  \] b) Diagonalize $A$ and find a formula for $A^n$
c) Find a formula for $\phi_n$.

记 $\lambda_1 = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}, \lambda_2 = -\frac{1}{\lambda_1} = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2},$
\[ \begin{aligned}
 A &= \begin{pmatrix} 
    1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 
\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 
    \lambda_1 & \lambda_2 \\ 1 & 1 
\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 
    \lambda_1 & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_2
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 
    \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & -\frac{\lambda_2}{\sqrt{5}} \\ -\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{\lambda_1}{\sqrt{5}}
\end{pmatrix} \\
A^n &= \begin{pmatrix} 
    \lambda_1 & \lambda_2 \\ 1 & 1 
\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 
    \lambda_1^n & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_2^n
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 
    \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & -\frac{\lambda_2}{\sqrt{5}} \\ -\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{\lambda_1}{\sqrt{5}}
\end{pmatrix} \\
    &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \begin{pmatrix}
    \lambda_1^{n+1} - \lambda_2^{n+1} & \lambda_1^n - \lambda_2^n \\ \lambda_1^n - \lambda_2^n & \lambda_1^{n-1} - \lambda_2^{n-1}
\end{pmatrix}
\end{aligned} \] 由 \[ \begin{pmatrix} 
    \phi_{n+2} \\ \phi_{n+1} 
\end{pmatrix} = A^n \begin{pmatrix} 
    \phi_2 \\ \phi_1 
\end{pmatrix} = A^n \begin{pmatrix} 
    1 \\ 0 
\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 
    \phi_{n+2} & \phi_{n+1} \\ \phi_{n+1} & \phi_n
\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 
    1 \\ 0 
\end{pmatrix}, \] 得 $\phi_n = \frac{\lambda_1^{n-1}-\lambda_2^{n-1}}{\sqrt{5}}$

>> d) Show that the vector $(\phi_{n+1}/\phi_{n},1)^T$ converges to an eigenvector of $A$. What do you think, is it a coincidence?

注意到 $|\lambda_1|>1,|\lambda_2|<1$，$\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\frac{\phi_{n+1}}{\phi_n} = \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\frac{\lambda_1^n-\lambda_2^n}{\lambda_1^{n-1}-\lambda_2^{n-1}} = \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\frac{\lambda_1^n}{\lambda_1^{n-1}} = \lambda_1$，而 $(\lambda_1, 1)^T$ 为 $A$ 的特征向量之一。
称矩阵 $A$ 所有特征值中模最大者为主特征值（这里是 $\lambda_1$）。对于任意初始向量 $\mathbf{x_0} = \sum^n_{i=1} c_i \mathbf{v}_i$，其中 $\mathbf{v}_i$ 为 $A$ 的特征向量，易得 $\lim_{n \to \infty}A^n \mathbf{x}_0 = \lim_{n \to \infty} c_k \lambda_k^n \mathbf{v}_k$，其中 $\lambda_k$ 为主特征值。

> 2.13 Eigenvalues of a transposition:
>> b) Consider the transformation $T$ in the space $M_{n\times n}$, $T(A) = A^T$. Find all its eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Is it possible to diagonalize this transformation?

交换矩阵 (commutation matrix) $K^{(n,n)} = \sum^n_{i=1}\sum^n_{j=1}(E_{i,j}\otimes E_{j,i})$（$n\times n$ 分块矩阵，第 $i,j$ 块为单位矩阵 $E_{j,i}$）。
由 $T^2 = I$ 知其特征值只能为 $\pm 1$，观察知 $\lambda_1 = 1$ 对应特征向量为对称矩阵 $S_n$，$\lambda_2 = -1$ 对应反对称矩阵 $A_n$，前者由上三角部分与对角线上元素决定，$\dim S_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$，后者由上三角部分决定，对角线上为 $0$，$\dim A_n = \frac{n(n-1)}{2}$. $\dim S_n + \dim A_n = \dim M_{n\times n} = n^2$，故 $T$ 可对角化。对角化后为 $$\Lambda = \begin{pmatrix} I_{k_1} & 0 \\ 0 & -I_{k_2} \end{pmatrix} = \text{diag}(\underbrace{1, \dots, 1}_{\frac{n(n+1)}{2}}, \underbrace{-1, \dots, -1}_{\frac{n(n-1)}{2}}),$$ 相应特征基为 $E_{i,i}, E_{i,j} + E_{j,i}, E_{i,j}-E_{j,i}，(i<j)$
任何方阵 $M$ 都可以唯一地分解为一个对称矩阵和一个反对称矩阵之和：\[ M = \underbrace{\frac{M + M^T}{2}}_{\in S_n} + \underbrace{\frac{M - M^T}{2}}_{\in A_n} \]
